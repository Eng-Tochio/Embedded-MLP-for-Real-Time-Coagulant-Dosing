# Embedded MLP for Real-Time Coagulant Dosing

Repository for the article:

**Embedded MLP for Real-Time Coagulant Dosing: From Systematic Model Selection to Field Deployment at a Full-Scale Water Treatment Plant**

## Overview

This repository contains the supplementary materials, source files, scripts, and/or documentation associated with the article on the development and field deployment of an embedded multilayer perceptron (MLP) model for real-time coagulant dosing in a full-scale water treatment plant.

The work focuses on the systematic selection of machine learning models, embedded implementation, and practical validation under real operating conditions in water treatment infrastructure.

## Research Context

Coagulant dosing is a critical operational step in conventional water treatment processes. Manual or rule-based dosing strategies may be affected by variability in raw water quality, operational constraints, and delayed laboratory feedback.

This study investigates the use of an embedded MLP model as a decision-support or automation component for real-time coagulant dosing. The workflow includes model selection, validation, embedded deployment, and field evaluation in a full-scale water treatment plant.

## Main Contributions

- Systematic comparison and selection of machine learning models for coagulant dosing.
- Development of an embedded MLP approach for real-time operation.
- Deployment-oriented evaluation under full-scale water treatment conditions.
- Practical discussion of implementation constraints, reliability, and field applicability.
